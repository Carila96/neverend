## 2. Copyright Infringement — DMCA Notice Procedure

If you believe that content displayed through neverEND infringes your copyright, you may submit a takedown notice to Carila.

### 2.1 What to Include in a Takedown Notice

Your notice must include all of the following:

1. **Your contact information:** Full legal name, address, telephone number, and email address
2. **Identification of the copyrighted work:** A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed, or a representative list if multiple works are involved
3. **Identification of the infringing content:** A description of the content you claim is infringing and sufficient information to locate it (e.g., which stage, approximate grid location)
4. **Good faith statement:** A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the content is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
5. **Accuracy statement:** A statement that the information in your notice is accurate and, under penalty of perjury, that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the owner's behalf
6. **Signature:** Your physical or electronic signature

### 2.2 Where to Send Notices

Submit copyright takedown notices to:

**Carila — IP / Copyright Notice**
Email: [IP email]
Subject line: "DMCA Takedown Notice"

### 2.3 What Happens After We Receive a Notice

Upon receipt of a valid takedown notice:

1. We will review the notice promptly (typically within **2 business days**)
2. If the notice is valid, we will remove or disable access to the identified content
3. We will notify the content uploader of the removal and the reason
4. The uploader may submit a counter-notice (see Section 3)

Submitting a false or fraudulent takedown notice may result in legal liability.

## 3. Counter-Notice Procedure

If you believe your content was removed due to a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice.

### 3.1 What to Include in a Counter-Notice

1. **Your contact information:** Full legal name, address, telephone number, and email address
2. **Identification of the removed content:** Description of the content that was removed and its location before removal
3. **Good faith statement:** A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the content was removed due to mistake or misidentification
4. **Consent to jurisdiction:** A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for your location (if in the USA), or the courts of Japan (if outside the USA), and that you will accept service of process from the original complainant
5. **Signature:** Your physical or electronic signature

### 3.2 Where to Send Counter-Notices

Submit counter-notices to:

**Carila — IP Counter-Notice**
Email: [IP email]
Subject line: "DMCA Counter-Notice"

### 3.3 What Happens After We Receive a Counter-Notice

1. We will forward the counter-notice to the original complainant
2. If we do not receive notice that the complainant has filed a court action within **10–14 business days**, we may restore the removed content at our discretion
